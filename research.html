Research

My current work focuses on how right-wing digital propaganda circulates on platforms like Twitter (X) in the Indian context. I am interested in the structural and emotional patterns behind these campaigns — how they frame political reality, mobilize attention, and obscure accountability.

This project draws on both machine learning techniques and critical perspectives on media, ideology, and power. I approach digital systems not just as technical infrastructures, but as cultural and political environments that shape the way people see, speak, and act.

I am also attentive to how mechanisms of classification, recommendation, and behavioral targeting operate within these platforms — often quietly reinforcing dominant narratives and forms of control. These dynamics blur the line between influence and observation, raising deeper questions about visibility, freedom, and consent in digital life.

At a broader level, I seek to explore how technical systems encode political intent, and how we might develop better ways of reading — and resisting — them.
