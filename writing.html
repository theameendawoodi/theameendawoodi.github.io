2.Some Thoughts on privacy

In Michel Foucault's panopticon, regulating self in certain way could mean that the panopticon is successful in itself and being anonymous in this digital age is to hide our packets and requests in other internet traffic like with Tor and VPN now, the dilemma is that should we move to anonymous and privacy based services and make the existence of panopticon success full or reject the unethical surveillance onto us, maybe the answer to this dilemma starts with some questions, does complete anonymity online and offline exist in this digitally heavy world. I mean, it's kinda possible for us to achieve anonymity online with encrypted mail, messages, VPNs, Tor network and hardened, sandboxed and virtual machine Linux like Qubes with Whonix or customised Arch Linux for privacy. If you were thinking of going LFS, dude, just why?. And good for you, maybe I'll be you someday, I guess. But going anonymous offline seems near impossible, Aadhaar itself is a big tool for the panopticon, and you miss out on many things without Aadhaar, so the question remains, is going full anonymous like ed snowden worth it...?
